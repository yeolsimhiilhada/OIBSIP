Biography

Steven Paul "Steve" Jobs was an American information technology entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc., one of the most influential technology companies in the world.He was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, and was adopted shortly after his birth. Jobs attended Reed College but dropped out after six months to pursue his passion for technology. In 1976, he co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. in his parents' garage along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne.He was the cofounder,chairman and CEO of Apple Inc , CEO and largest stakeholder of Pixar Animation Studios , and , also founder , chairman and CEO of NeXt Inc.He was known for his visionary leadership style, attention to detail, and relentless pursuit of perfection. In 1985, Jobs was ousted from Apple following a power struggle with the company's board of directors. He went on to found NeXT Inc., a computer hardware and software company. Jobs returned to Apple in 1997 when the company acquired NeXT. He became CEO and spearheaded a remarkable turnaround, introducing groundbreaking products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad.Under his leadership, Apple became the world's most valuable company and revolutionized multiple industries, including music, telecommunications, and personal computing. Jobs was known for his charismatic personality and captivating presentation style, often referred to as "reality distortion field." He passed away on October 5, 2011, after a long battle with pancreatic cancer, leaving behind a lasting legacy as one of the greatest innovators and visionaries of our time.
